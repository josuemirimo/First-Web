The idea to start TOMORROW VIJANA (Tomorrow's Youth) emerged after the two founders, Kubana Alexis and Mathias Rukira, identified language barrier as being the number one barrier encountered by the refugee community through intensive research in the Humanitarian Innovation Project through Oxford University in 2014. It became evident that overcoming language barriers would allow refugees to successfully integrate into the mainstream Ugandan community. Tomorrow Vijanaâ€™s educational & skills training programs allow refugees to become proficient in English, the official language of Uganda, & obtain skills to become self sufficient, improving their overall quality of life. Since Tomorrow Vijana's inception in 2014, the organization has grown from holding classes with students seated outdoors, under a tree to having a permanent Community Learning Center.
